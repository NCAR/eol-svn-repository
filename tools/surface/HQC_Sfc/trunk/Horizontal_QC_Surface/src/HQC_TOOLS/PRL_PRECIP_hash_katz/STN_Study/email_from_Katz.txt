> Here is more than you care to know about the station list which I sent on the
> archive tapes.  That list has not been updated for at least 2 years.  It seems

> The format for the 'official' NWS ID is 3 characters for the site with a 2
> character state
> abbreviation.  The state abbreviation is a letter (first letter of the
> state in question)
> followed by a number (alphabetical order).  Calif is C1, Colo is C2, and
> Conn is C3.
> The other 'official' NWS ID is the well known ID used at the NWS 1st order
> type
> stations, ie DEN for Denver(old airport), GJT Grand Junction, etc.
> However, there
> are other program ID's associated with the site ID, such as NCDC cooperative
> site number, NESDIS GOES platform ID.  Therefore, GJT can have a cooperative
> reporting site and a GOES platform which will lead to extra entries in the
> file nwsli.uniq
> And I have thrown is a few hundred ID's from specialized networks that
> don't follow
> any rules.  The SHEF decoder accepts any combination of 3 to 8 characters
> as a valid
> ID.  I suspect that an ID of 4 letters is not valid, whether all letters or
> 3 letters
> and a digit.  I have noticed that the hourly precipitation files tend to
> have a lot of these
> 4 character ID's.  Garbled messages is a probable cause.  I will try to
> look into this
> during the next couple of weeks.
> 
> Another explanation for missing ID's is the use of  'stranger' reports in a
> SHEF
> encoded message.  Quoting from the NWS SHEF Manual, "Stranger reports are
> reports form unofficial locations for which no 5-character identifier
> exists.  Such
> reports can come form a variety of sources, including radio and TV
> stations, law
> enforcement agencies, etc.  Even though such reports are infrequently
> received,
> it may at times be important to have them deciphered by the SHEF decoder and
> perhaps stored in a database.'   The format is Xxxxyyyy, where xxx is the
> latitude
> and yyyy is the longitude of the site. E.G.  X3080995 is 30.8 degrees north
> latitude
> and 99.5 degrees west longitude.
>
